About Me

Hi, I'm Cheng Peng

New York | Beijing

MS Design Technology at Cornell University, US

BA Architecture at University of Manchester, UK

With a background in architecture and a focus on design technology, I approach design through the lens of systems, interaction, and spatial logic. I’m currently pursuing a Master’s in Design Technology at Cornell University, and hold a Bachelor’s in Architecture (RIBA Part I) from the University of Manchester. My work integrates physical space with digital systems to explore adaptive, responsive design.

Previously, I worked as an Architectural Assistant at China IPPR International Engineering, where I contributed to the design of large-scale healthcare projects - supporting concept development, spatial planning, and technical documentation in collaboration with multidisciplinary teams.

As a designer navigating the intersection of architecture and emerging technology, I value iterative thinking, inclusive collaboration, and purposeful experimentation. I am committed to creating thoughtful, future-forward design solutions that merge physical space with digital intelligence.

TimberLift

Robotic Fabrication

1 / 6

TimberLift is a research-based fabrication project that investigates robotic machining strategies for optimizing timber joints in cantilevered furniture. Using an ABB robotic arm and stress-informed cylindrical joints, the project enhances structural performance while reducing material waste through standardized double-sided machining and adaptive grain alignment.
